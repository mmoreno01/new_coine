Alliances

COINE has achieved agreements to reach desired objectives and to create alliances to benefit the society

Aiming to break the ideological barriers and homogenizing the cooperation efforts between the nations of Asia, Middle East and Oceania, the International Conference of Asian Political Parties (ICAPP) was created, whose essence is based on the understanding and trust among Asian nations, with the purpose of disseminating mutual cooperation in that region of the world.

ICAPP has been successful since September 2000 when it emerged in Manila, Singapore, achieving exponential growth that has allowed its partners to jointly create international policies on the welfare of their nations, as well as their relationship with other regions and / or continents..

By 2017, ICAPP has the support of more than 350 political parties from 52 Asian and Oceanian nations, it has held nine General Assemblies and plays a key role not only in the construction of a cohesive community in Asia, but in the configuration of greater alternatives to combat the specific problems of each country in the area.
Taking as an example the achievements in Latin America and Asia, in 2013 a conglomerate of African political parties created in Sudan the Council of African Political Parties (CAPP), whose premises, like their continental counterparts, were instituted in the agreements of mutual cooperation, to address the main social-political problems of the African continent.

Its members elected Winter Kamimba as their first president, granting him a place on the Standing Committee; In addition to this, the Sudanese government designated a building in the city of Khartoum to serve as the headquarters of this Council.
